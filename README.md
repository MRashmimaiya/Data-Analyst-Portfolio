 COVID-19 Global Operations & Revenue Impact
 Project Overview
This project analyzes the spread of COVID-19 across India and Italy.

 Tools Used
* SQL:Data aggregation and rate calculations.
* Excel:Interactive slicer-based dashboard.
* Python:Time-series visualization.

* Key Insight: Growth Rate & Peak Analysis
* Through time-series analysis in Power BI, I identified a significant difference in the "acceleration" of cases between regions:
* Early Surge in India: India experienced a much steeper initial growth rate, reaching its peak of 445 daily cases within the first 5 days of March. This suggests a high-density transmission event or a rapid update in reporting at the start of the period.
* Sustained Climb in Italy: In contrast, Italy showed a more gradual progression, peaking later on March 30th. This allowed for a comparison of how different regions' "curves" behaved—essential for hospital resource planning and operational readiness.

The Comparison: India vs. Italy
When comparing the two countries in your Power BI Line Chart:
India shows a steeper, more immediate peak at the very beginning of the dataset (March 2020). It reached its highest daily case count in this file (445 cases) on March 5th, showing a very sharp vertical rise right at the start.
Italy shows a more gradual and sustained climb. It reached its peak in this dataset (407 cases) later in the month on March 30th. While its total volume was significant, the "slope" of the line was less vertical than India's initial surge.
